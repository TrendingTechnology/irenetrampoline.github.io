MIT Clinical Machine Learning Group

Led by David Sontag, the Clinical Machine Learning Group is broadly interested in advancing machine learning and artificial intelligence, and using these techniques to advance health care.

Recent advances in machine learning have shown tremendous success, but deployment in high-stakes settings are still limited. We believe that healthcare is an excellent setting for studying these research problems---including but not limited to causality, generalizability, and fairness and interpretability---and finding rigorous solutions, which would help pave the way for safe deployment of machine learning beyond healthcare.

From a clinical perspective, deploying machine learning in real-world healthcare settings is challenging, and requires deep clinical expertise and intuition. To that end, we work with clinical collaborators to grow beyond publishing paperes and identify clinical questions, develop novel methods, and evaluation our solutions in clinical contexts. In order to truly make a difference in health care, we need to demonstrate that they are practically useful for solving real clinical problems.
